### Company Overview

UnionBank of the Philippines is one of the country's top universal banks, offering a full range of financial products and services including retail banking, corporate banking, investment banking, and trust services. It is a member of the **Aboitiz Group**, one of the Philippines' largest conglomerates.

UnionBank is widely regarded as the **most digitally advanced bank in the Philippines**. It has received multiple awards for digital innovation and has consistently invested in transforming its operations through technology. It was among the first Philippine banks to launch a fully digital banking platform and to integrate blockchain-based services in its operations.

---

### Digital Strategy and IT Infrastructure

UnionBank's digital transformation strategy is built on three pillars:

1. **Customer Experience** — Delivering seamless banking services through digital channels, including the UnionBank Online app and the EON digital banking platform.
2. **Operational Efficiency** — Automating internal processes using artificial intelligence, robotic process automation (RPA), and cloud computing.
3. **Innovation and Partnerships** — Expanding through its fintech subsidiary **UBX Philippines**, which provides open finance infrastructure for third-party developers and partner institutions.

Key technology investments include:

- **Cloud adoption** — Migration of core banking workloads to hybrid cloud environments
- **API banking** — Open API framework enabling fintech integrations through UBX
- **AI and Data Analytics** — Use of machine learning for credit scoring, fraud detection, and customer personalization
- **Blockchain** — Pilot programs for cross-border remittances and supply chain finance

### Regulatory Environment

As a BSP-supervised entity, UnionBank is subject to a comprehensive set of IT-related regulations:

- **BSP Circular No. 982** (2017) — Mandates IT risk management frameworks for banks
- **BSP Circular No. 1140** (2022) — Requires governance of digital operations, cloud services, and third-party technology providers
- **Republic Act No. 10173** (Data Privacy Act of 2012) — Governs the collection, processing, and protection of personal data
- **BSP Circular No. 1048** (2019) — Guidelines on financial consumer protection in digital channels

### Why UnionBank Was Selected

UnionBank is an ideal subject for this IT governance proposal for the following reasons:

1. **High digital exposure** — Its heavy reliance on digital infrastructure increases the impact of IT governance failures.
2. **Regulatory complexity** — Multiple BSP circulars directly mandate IT governance structures, providing clear benchmarks for assessment.
3. **Public disclosures** — As a publicly listed company, UnionBank publishes annual reports, integrated reports, and regulatory disclosures that allow for informed analysis.
4. **Known security incidents** — Documented cybersecurity events (e.g., the 2021 phishing incidents affecting customers) provide concrete case studies for governance gap analysis.
5. **Active fintech ecosystem** — Its third-party integrations through UBX create a vendor risk surface that is relevant to modern IT governance frameworks.
