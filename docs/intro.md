# IT Policy and Governance Proposal

**Organization:** UnionBank of the Philippines
**Document Type:** IT Governance Proposal
**Framework:** COBIT 2019
**Version:** 1.0
**Date:** April 17, 2026
**Prepared for:** Information Assurance and Security 2 — Final Project

---

## Executive Summary

This document presents a proposed IT Policy and Governance framework for **UnionBank of the Philippines (UBP)**, one of the country's leading universal banks and a recognized leader in digital banking transformation.

As digital operations become increasingly central to UnionBank's business model, the need for a robust, structured, and formally documented IT governance framework becomes critical — not only for operational resilience, but also for compliance with Philippine regulatory mandates issued by the **Bangko Sentral ng Pilipinas (BSP)** and the **National Privacy Commission (NPC)**.

This proposal was developed using **COBIT 2019** (Control Objectives for Information and Related Technologies) as the primary governance framework, published by ISACA. COBIT 2019 is the internationally recognized standard for IT governance and management, and its governance objectives directly align with the gaps identified in UnionBank's current IT governance posture.

---

## Scope

This proposal covers the following governance domains for UnionBank of the Philippines:

- IT risk governance and board-level reporting
- Third-party and cloud vendor risk management
- Cybersecurity incident response and post-incident review

It applies to all business units, employees, contractors, and third-party service providers that access, process, or manage UnionBank's information systems and data.

## Summary of Identified Gaps and Proposed Policies

| # | Identified Gap | COBIT 2019 Objective | Proposed Policy |
|---|----------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| 1 | Reactive board-level IT risk reporting | EDM03 — Ensured Risk Optimization | IT Risk Management and Board Reporting Policy |
| 2 | No formalized vendor and cloud risk policy | APO10 — Managed Vendors | Third-Party and Cloud Vendor Risk Management Policy |
| 3 | No post-incident review process for cybersecurity events | DSS02 — Managed Service Requests and Incidents | Cybersecurity Incident Response and Post-Incident Review Policy |

## Regulatory Basis

This proposal is aligned with the following Philippine regulations:

- **BSP Circular No. 982** (2017) — Guidelines on Information Technology Risk Management
- **BSP Circular No. 1140** (2022) — Digital Operations Framework
- **Republic Act No. 10173** — Data Privacy Act of 2012
