## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes a formal, structured, and **proactive** process for identifying, assessing, communicating, and monitoring IT-related risks at UnionBank of the Philippines. It ensures that the Board of Directors and senior management are provided with timely, accurate, and actionable information to make informed decisions on IT risk — thereby transforming IT risk governance from a reactive to a proactive function.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- All members of the Board of Directors and the Board IT Steering Committee
- The Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) and information security team
- The Chief Information Officer (CIO) and IT management
- All business units that own, operate, or rely on IT systems
- Internal audit functions responsible for IT risk review

---

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **IT Risk** | The potential for loss or harm related to technical infrastructure, information systems, or data that supports the organization's business objectives |
| **Risk Register** | A formal document listing identified risks, their likelihood, impact, risk owner, and current mitigation status |
| **Risk Appetite** | The amount and type of IT risk the board is willing to accept in pursuit of business objectives |
| **Risk Tolerance** | The acceptable deviation from the defined risk appetite |
| **Risk Rating** | A classification of risk severity: Low, Medium, High, or Critical |
| **Key Risk Indicator (KRI)** | A metric that provides early warning of increasing IT risk exposure |

---

## 4. Policy Statements

### 4.1 Risk Appetite Statement

4.1.1 The Board IT Steering Committee **shall** formally approve an **IT Risk Appetite Statement** on an annual basis. This statement defines the nature and level of IT risk that UnionBank is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives.

4.1.2 The IT Risk Appetite Statement **shall** be reviewed and updated whenever there is a material change in the organization's business strategy, technology environment, or regulatory requirements.

4.1.3 All IT risk management activities **shall** be aligned with the approved risk appetite.

### 4.2 IT Risk Register

4.2.1 The CISO **shall** maintain a centralized **IT Risk Register** containing all identified IT risks across the organization.

4.2.2 Each entry in the IT Risk Register **shall** include:
- Risk ID and title
- Risk description
- Risk category (e.g., cybersecurity, operational, compliance, third-party)
- Likelihood rating (1–5 scale)
- Impact rating (1–5 scale)
- Overall risk rating (Low / Medium / High / Critical)
- Risk owner (accountable individual or unit)
- Current control or mitigation measure
- Residual risk after controls
- Target resolution date (for High/Critical risks)
- Status (Open / In Progress / Resolved / Accepted)

4.2.3 The IT Risk Register **shall** be reviewed and updated at a minimum on a **quarterly** basis, and immediately following any significant IT incident or material change to the technology environment.

### 4.3 Board-Level Risk Reporting

4.3.1 The CISO **shall** submit a **Quarterly IT Risk Report** to the Board IT Steering Committee. The report **shall** include:
- Executive summary of the current IT risk posture
- Summary of the IT Risk Register (by category and severity)
- Status of previously reported High/Critical risks
- New risks identified during the reporting period
- Key Risk Indicator (KRI) trend analysis
- Significant IT incidents that occurred during the period
- Recommended board actions or decisions required

4.3.2 The Board IT Steering Committee **shall** formally review, discuss, and acknowledge receipt of the Quarterly IT Risk Report at each scheduled board meeting.

4.3.3 Minutes of the board's review of the IT Risk Report **shall** be documented and retained for a minimum of **five (5) years**.

### 4.4 Escalation of High and Critical Risks

4.4.1 Any IT risk newly identified or elevated to a rating of **High** or **Critical shall** be escalated to the CISO and CIO within **24 hours** of identification.

4.4.2 The CISO **shall** notify the Board IT Steering Committee of any High or Critical risk within **48 hours** of identification, providing:
- Risk description and potential business impact
- Immediate mitigation actions taken or planned
- Recommended board direction

4.4.3 A formal risk response plan for all High and Critical risks **shall** be submitted to the Board IT Steering Committee within **10 business days**.

## 5. Roles and Responsibilities

| Role | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| **Board IT Steering Committee** | Approve IT Risk Appetite Statement; review quarterly risk reports; direct risk responses for High/Critical risks |
| **CISO** | Own and maintain the IT Risk Register; prepare quarterly risk reports; escalate High/Critical risks; coordinate risk response |
| **CIO** | Ensure IT operations align with approved risk appetite; support CISO in risk identification and mitigation |
| **Business Unit Heads** | Identify and report IT risks within their units; implement assigned risk mitigation actions |
| **Internal Audit** | Independently assess the effectiveness of the IT risk management process; report findings to the board |
| **Risk Management Office** | Coordinate enterprise-wide risk management; ensure alignment between IT risk and overall enterprise risk framework |

## 6. Risk Rating Matrix

Risk ratings are determined by combining likelihood and impact scores:

| | **Impact: Low (1–2)** | **Impact: Medium (3)** | **Impact: High (4–5)** |
|---|----------------------|----------------------|----------------------|
| **Likelihood: High (4–5)** | Medium | High | **Critical** |
| **Likelihood: Medium (3)** | Low | Medium | High |
| **Likelihood: Low (1–2)** | Low | Low | Medium |

**Definitions:**
- **Critical** — Immediate board notification and response required within 48 hours
- **High** — Board notification required; response plan within 10 business days
- **Medium** — Managed through regular IT operations; reported quarterly
- **Low** — Logged and monitored; no immediate escalation required

---

## 7. Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

The following KRIs **shall** be tracked and included in quarterly board reports:

| KRI | Threshold (Alert) | Threshold (Critical) |
|-----|------------------|---------------------|
| Number of open Critical IT risks | ≥ 1 | ≥ 3 |
| Number of overdue High/Critical risk mitigations | ≥ 2 | ≥ 5 |
| Number of significant security incidents per quarter | ≥ 3 | ≥ 5 |
| Percentage of IT assets without completed risk assessment | ≥ 15% | ≥ 30% |
| Vendor risk assessments overdue | ≥ 2 | ≥ 5 |

## 8. Compliance and Enforcement

8.1 Compliance with this policy is mandatory for all covered roles defined in Section 2.

8.2 Non-compliance by individual employees may result in disciplinary action up to and including termination, in accordance with UnionBank's Human Resources policies.

8.3 The Internal Audit division **shall** include an assessment of this policy's implementation in its annual IT audit plan.

8.4 This policy shall be reviewed for compliance with BSP Circular No. 982 on a periodic basis and updated as needed to reflect regulatory changes.
